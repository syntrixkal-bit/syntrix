What we look for

The weaknesses attackers find first — found first by us.

You don't need to be a security expert. Every check below is something we assess, verify by hand, and explain in language anyone can act on.

Weak WiFi encryption

Outdated standards like WEP or WPA — or misconfigured WPA2/WPA3 — leave your traffic readable to anyone nearby.

Weak passwords

Default, short or reused passwords on your WiFi and router admin panel are the first thing an attacker tries.

Outdated router settings

Old firmware and forgotten settings accumulate known vulnerabilities that are trivially easy to look up — and exploit.

Unknown connected devices

Devices you don't recognise on your network may be freeloaders — or something worse. We identify every one.

Unsafe configurations

Remote admin access, WPS, UPnP and open ports — convenience features that quietly widen your attack surface.

Poor network segmentation

Guest devices, smart-home gadgets and work machines all on one flat network means one weak device exposes everything.

Coverage weaknesses

Signal that leaks far beyond your walls invites outsiders; dead zones push people onto insecure workarounds.

Potential attack surfaces

Every exposed service, port and entry point an attacker could reach — mapped, assessed and explained.

Every audit covers the categories relevant to your environment — and if something is in good shape, your report will say so. We don’t inflate findings to justify the fee.
